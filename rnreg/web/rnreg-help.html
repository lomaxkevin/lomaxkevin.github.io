ResNet Registration Help

You must register a device before it will function on ResNet. When an unregistered device connects to a jack the network electronics assign its port to a special network segment which has connectivity only to the registration server. After a device is registered and it is restarted, its port will be reassigned to a routed ResNet segment.

Log in

You must log in before you can register a machine. Log in with the credentials you use to log in to the network in the labs.

Your session will expire after 15 minutes of inactivity and you will need to log in again.

Register

If you are registering a device from a ResNet jack, the system should automatically determine your Ethernet address and operating system. If it is necessary to enter your Ethernet address manually, you can find it by running winipcfg on Win9x systems, running ipconfig /all on WinNT, Win2k and WinXP systems, or by accessing the AppleTalk or TCP/IP control panel on Macintosh systems. The Ethernet address consists of 12 hex digits and contains no delimiting colons or dashes, e.g. 0000e8129245. This address must be correct in the database or your machine will not be able to connect to ResNet.

Click the Agree & submit button to register your device. You will be held responsible for any violations of the appropriate use policy by devices registered under your name. There is a limit of three registrations per user.

If there are no errors, the registration system will return a hostname for your device. This hostname uniquely identifies your computer. Your IP address will change, but normally your hostname will remain the same.

Several of the ResNet tables are only updated every 15 minutes, so you may need to wait for your newly registered device to function on the network. After registering, shut down your system and wait until the next quarter hour before restarting and attempting to access the network. On Win95 systems it may be necessary to initiate a DHCP release and renew via winipcfg after the reboot.

Edit

This option permits you to edit or delete records of machines you have registered. You will need to edit a record if you have replaced your Ethernet card. Use the Next and Previous buttons to cycle through the hosts registered to you. Use the Update button to save changes you make. Use the Delete button to delete a device you no longer own. Updates and deletes are written to the database immediately.
