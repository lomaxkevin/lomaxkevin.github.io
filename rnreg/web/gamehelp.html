Finding your MAC Address


An example of a MAC Address is: 00:0D:0A:1H:3G:5C


Finding the Xbox One MAC Address:

These instructions are for configuring a Microsoft Xbox One for use with ResNet.

  1. Press the "Menu" button on your controller.
  2. Navigate to the Settings page.
  3. Select Network.
  4. Select Advanced Settings.
  5. The MAC Addresses for the wired and wireless adapters should be displayed.

Finding the PlayStation 4 MAC Address:

These instructions are for configuring a Sony PlayStation 4 for use with ResNet.

  1. On the PS4: Go to "Setttings"
  2. Scroll down to "System"
  3. Scroll down to "System Information"
  4. At the bottom you will find the "MAC Address (Wi-Fi)" which you will need to write down.

Finding the Wii U MAC Address:

These instructions are for configuring a Nintendo WIi for use with ResNet.

  1. On your game pad, select System Settings on the Wii U Menu
  2. Select "Internet"
  3. Select "View Mac Address"
  4. Write down the "MAC address".

Finding the Apple TV MAC Address:

These instructions are for configuring a Apple TV for use with ResNet.

  1. With your Apple TV remote, navigate to the Settings icon and select it.
  2. From the Settings menu, select General.
  3. Under General, select Network.
  4. Your MAC Address will be next to either Wi-Fi Address or Ethernet Address depending on whether you are connected to the internet via Wi-Fi or a Wired connection.

Finding the Roku MAC Address:

These instructions are for configuring a Roku for use with ResNet.

  1. On the back on your Roku there are a pair of six 2-digit pairs separated by colons, e.g. 00:0D:0A:1H:3G:5C. One set is for the wired conneciton and the other is for a wireless connection. You will want the wireless MAC Address.
  2. Alternatively, from the Roku home screen, select settings> player info. The MAC Addresses will be listed on-screen.

Finding the Smart TV/Bluray Player MAC Address:

These instructions are for configuring a Smart TV/Bluray Player for use with ResNet.

  1. Most MAC Addresses will be visible in the Settings Menu, either under the Network or About sections.
  2. Alternatively, check with the manufacturer for instructions on how to obtain the MAC Address for your specific model.

Once you have the MAC address, plug the network cable into a computer that has already been registered on ResNet via wired connection (ethernet) and register your Gaming Device using the Wireless Device Registration Portal
